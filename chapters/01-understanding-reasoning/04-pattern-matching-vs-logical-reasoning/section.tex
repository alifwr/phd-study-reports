A critical distinction exists between how LLMs "reason" and how deterministic rule-based systems operate.

\begin{itemize}
  \item Rule-Based Systems (Symbolic Logic): These follow strict, hand-crafted heuristics and formal rules. They guarantee logical consistency and correctness if the premises are true.
  \item Statistical LLMs (Pattern Matching): These identify statistical associations. For example, if a model says the capital of Germany is Berlin, it is recalling a high-probability association, not deducing it.
  \item Implicit vs. Explicit Reasoning: Conventional models like GPT-4o can simulate reasoning in familiar contexts because they have encountered similar scenarios in their training data.
\end{itemize}

 However, they struggle with novel scenarios or highly intricate multi-step relationships because they do not explicitly track logic or contradictions.
