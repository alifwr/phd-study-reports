\subsection{Inference-Time vs. Training-Time Scaling}

Reasoning performance and answer accuracy can be improved through two distinct compute investment strategies:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Inference-Time Scaling:} Increases accuracy by spending more computation per generated answer (e.g., advanced text generation and voting).
  \item \textbf{Training-Time Scaling:} Improves accuracy by investing additional computation during the training phase to modify model weights.
\end{itemize}


\subsection{The Role of Reinforcement Learning (RL)}

RL is typically applied as a post-training stage following pre-training and instruction fine-tuning.

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Pre-training:} Primarily builds the model's knowledge base by training it to predict the next token.
  \item \textbf{Reinforcement Learning:} Shapes how the model uses its knowledge, specifically focusing on its reasoning behavior. It allows for the optimization of whole outputs (e.g., answer correctness) rather than individual tokens.
\end{itemize}
